// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project images—Seattle Aquarium Ocean Pavilion, King County Metro Interim Base Electrification, McKee's Beach Drinking Water Treatment Plant—are almost certainly being served as unnamed, untagged decorative files with zero structured data attached. No JSON-LD Project schema, no geotagged EXIF coordinates, no descriptive alt-text tying those assets to location-specific queries means you are invisible for searches like "construction stormwater treatment contractor Bellevue WA." A competitor with properly structured image assets and local business schema on the same project types is capturing that traffic while your portfolio sits mute.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your environmental compliance buyers—project engineers and field managers at firms like Sellen Construction—need visual documentation of systems running in the field before they sign a lease or purchase contract. You have one testimonial from Andrew Spelman and a list of project names, but zero site photography showing deployed electrocoagulation or PFAS treatment systems operating under real site conditions. Without photographic proof of operational complexity, your written claims of "turnkey design through field services" read as marketing copy, not technical credibility.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Regional water treatment competitors targeting Washington State DOT and municipal construction contracts are publishing structured project galleries with equipment-level photography, permit documentation timelines, and discharge compliance visuals tied to specific project phases. You are listing the Washington State Fire Training Academy wastewater project and the Uranium Cleanup solar-powered system as featured work, but without visual assets those are just addresses—any procurement coordinator cross-referencing vendors will weight a competitor who shows the system against one who names it.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a single structured shoot covering three to four active Washington deployments—capture equipment serial identification, discharge point conditions, control panel interfaces, and site context—and deploy every file with GPS-embedded EXIF data, descriptive alt-text formatted to project type and location, and JSON-LD schema marking each as a completed engineering project with service area and treatment category. This converts your existing project list from a passive reference into a geo-indexed, search-visible asset library that works as a pre-qualification tool before your sales team takes a single call.
